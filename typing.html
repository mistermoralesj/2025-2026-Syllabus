Why Typing Matters in Programming

Typing well matters when learning to code. Fast typing isn't required, but learning to touch-type on a QWERTY keyboard makes everything easier. It helps you stay focused, write cleaner code, and work more efficiently.

We start with the basics:

  • Learn where the keys are. If you're constantly looking down, you can't focus on your code.
  • Then build accuracy. Typos cause bugs. The fewer you make, the less time you spend debugging.
  • Speed comes last. Once your fingers know where to go, speed will come naturally.

Here's why typing is part of this course:

  • You'll write more code, faster. Touch-typing keeps your hands moving and your eyes on the screen.
  • You'll be more accurate. Clean code is easier to read and easier to fix.
  • You'll be more comfortable. Good posture and less strain mean you can code longer without getting tired.
  • You'll look more professional. No one wants to type like a t-rex in front of their classmates or worse, an employer.

We use QWERTY because it's the standard. Every school-issued computer is QWERTY, and most development environments are built around it. Later on, some programmers try layouts like Dvorak or Colemak for better ergonomics or symbol placement. But for now, QWERTY is what you'll use here and in most jobs.

Start by finding the keys. Then get accurate. Then get fast. Strong typing gives you an edge in programming and in your future professional occupation.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Go to the official ClassLink sign-in page for Osceola County. You can usually find it bookmarked or linked from NeoCity Academy's school homepage.
  2. Sign in with your school credentials.
  3. Once on the ClassLink dashboard, locate and click on the Typing.com app icon.
  4. This will automatically sign you into the district-managed account created for you.

Important Reminders

  • Do not go directly to Typing.com and create your own account. If you bypass ClassLink, your progress will not be recorded and will not count toward your grade.
  • You must use the official district-provided account, or Mr. Morales will not be able to see your results or track your performance.
  • Other typing platforms such as MonkeyType or Nitro Type are not allowed for class credit. These services do not connect to your official student account and will not be accepted for grading.
  • If Typing.com shows multiple teachers, make sure to select "Morales." Otherwise, your progress will not appear in the gradebook.

Your grade depends on using the correct system. Stay in ClassLink and use only the district-approved Typing.com app.

If you have trouble finding the Typing.com app in ClassLink, please let Mr. Morales know right away.
